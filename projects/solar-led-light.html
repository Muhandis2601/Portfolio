Solar LED Light

Solar-Powered LED Lamp Design & Fabrication

Sekolah Vokasi Universitas Gadjah Mada

About the Project

This project involved designing and building a solar-powered LED lamp as the final deliverable for the Technical Drawing 2 and Workshop Practice 2 courses. The project combined 3D modeling, electronic circuit design, and practical fabrication skills to produce a functional solar-charged lighting device.

Autodesk Inventor was used to design a detailed 3D model of the lamp casing, ensuring proper dimensions, structural integrity, and manufacturability. Eagle software was used to design the printed circuit board (PCB) for the electrical components, including the solar charging circuit, battery management, and LED driver.

The completed lamp is capable of recharging via a solar panel and also supports conventional cable charging as a backup option. This dual-mode charging system increases the lamp's practical usability across various lighting scenarios. The project provided hands-on experience in interdisciplinary engineering work spanning mechanical design, PCB design, and sustainable energy application.

Tools & Technologies

  • Autodesk Inventor — 3D modeling of the lamp casing
  • Eagle PCB — Printed circuit board design for electrical components
  • Solar Panel Integration — Solar charging circuit and battery management
  • PCB Fabrication — Physical board production and component assembly

Key Outcomes

  • Designed a complete 3D model of the solar LED lamp casing using Autodesk Inventor.
  • Created a fully functional PCB layout for the lamp's electrical circuit using Eagle software.
  • Built a working solar-powered LED lamp with both solar panel and conventional cable charging support.
  • Gained practical experience in combining mechanical design, PCB development, and renewable energy integration.
