Analysis of the Electric Power Protection System on Lombok Island

Substation Protection Design & Simulation

Sekolah Vokasi Universitas Gadjah Mada

About the Project

This final project was completed in collaboration with a colleague, focusing on developing a comprehensive and reliable electric power protection system for Lombok Island. The project encompassed designing subsystems of Grounding Indicators (GI) across several key substation locations, including Jeranjang, Ampenan, Mantang, Pulang Sengkol, Kuta, Selong, Pringgabayan, Bayan, and Tanjung.

Using ETAP software, the team engineered and simulated a comprehensive protection system to ensure optimal performance and reliability across these locations. The project placed emphasis on analyzing and selecting the most suitable protection systems, while also designing a robust electrical network that mitigates the risks of overvoltage and undervoltage through the use of capacitor banks on electrical buses.

This experience provided invaluable insights into protection system analysis and design, enhancing the ability to develop reliable and efficient electrical networks. It also reinforced the importance of teamwork and effective communication in tackling complex engineering challenges.

Tools & Technologies

  • ETAP — Power system simulation and protection analysis
  • Protection Analysis — Relay coordination and fault current calculation
  • Capacitor Bank Design — Voltage stability and reactive power compensation

Key Outcomes

  • Designed subsystem protection for 9 key substation locations across Lombok Island.
  • Simulated a complete protection system covering fault detection, overvoltage, and undervoltage scenarios.
  • Proposed capacitor bank placements to improve voltage stability across the distribution network.
