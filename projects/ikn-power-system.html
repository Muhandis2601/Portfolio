Designing and Developing Electric Power Systems in the IKN Area

Cost-Effective Power Infrastructure for Indonesia's New Capital

Sekolah Vokasi Universitas Gadjah Mada

About the Project

This project involved designing and developing electric power systems for the IKN (Ibu Kota Nusantara) area — Indonesia's new capital city — with a focus on identifying cost-effective solutions that balance budget constraints with optimal system performance. The project required a thorough analysis of the planned infrastructure landscape and projected future energy demands of the new capital.

A strategic master plan was developed that incorporated both renewable energy sources and advanced distribution technologies. The design process combined technical rigor with economic considerations, ensuring that the proposed power system architecture could realistically be delivered within the budgetary parameters of a large-scale national infrastructure project.

By focusing simultaneously on technical performance and financial feasibility, the project produced cost-optimized solutions that demonstrate both engineering competence and practical awareness of real-world project constraints. This experience enhanced the ability to develop sustainable, high-performance electrical systems in complex and budget-sensitive environments.

Tools & Technologies

  • Electrical Engineering — Power system design and load flow analysis
  • Electrical Wiring Design — Distribution network layout and cabling
  • Economic Analysis — Cost-benefit evaluation and budget optimization
  • Renewable Energy Integration — Solar and sustainable energy inclusion

Key Outcomes

  • Identified and proposed cost-effective power system configurations for the IKN development area.
  • Developed a master plan integrating renewable energy into the IKN distribution network.
  • Demonstrated the ability to balance technical system performance with real-world budget constraints.
