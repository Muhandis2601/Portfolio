Research Assistant Lecturer

Railway Track Vibration Sensor Development

Sekolah Vokasi Universitas Gadjah Mada

About the Project

In this role as a research assistant working closely with faculty members, the focus was on contributing to a groundbreaking research project aimed at developing and implementing vibration sensors to measure the magnitude of vibrations occurring on railway tracks as trains traverse them. This research lies at the intersection of engineering, sensor technology, and railway safety, with significant implications for improving rail infrastructure reliability and efficiency.

The primary objective was to design and deploy sensors capable of accurately quantifying the vibrations induced by moving trains. Responsibilities included collaborating with the research team to conceptualize, design, and fabricate these sensors. The work began with a comprehensive review of existing literature on vibration sensing and railway track monitoring, ensuring the research was built on a solid and up-to-date foundation.

The sensor development process required a multidisciplinary approach, combining principles from mechanical engineering, electronics, and data analytics. Working alongside experienced researchers and using state-of-the-art equipment, active participation in prototyping and testing various sensor designs helped refine accuracy, reliability, and adaptability to different railway environments. The role also involved designing the vibration sensor casing using Autodesk Inventor and programming the Arduino Uno for sensor data acquisition and analysis.

Tools & Technologies

  • Autodesk Inventor — 3D modeling and design of the vibration sensor casing
  • Arduino Uno — Microcontroller programming for sensor data acquisition
  • AutoCAD — Technical drawings and documentation
  • Electrical Engineering Principles — Sensor circuit design and signal processing

Key Outcomes

  • Designed and fabricated a vibration sensor casing optimized for railway track deployment using Autodesk Inventor.
  • Programmed the Arduino Uno to acquire, process, and analyze vibration sensor data from railway tracks.
  • Conducted performance testing on motor bearings to validate sensor accuracy and measurement reliability.
  • Contributed to research that aims to improve railway maintenance scheduling and infrastructure durability.
