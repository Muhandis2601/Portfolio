Project Overview

About the Project

In this role as a research assistant working closely with faculty members, the focus was on contributing to a groundbreaking research project aimed at developing and implementing vibration sensors to measure the magnitude of vibrations occurring on railway tracks as trains traverse them. This research lies at the intersection of engineering, sensor technology, and railway safety.

The primary objective was to design and deploy sensors capable of accurately quantifying the vibrations induced by moving trains. The process began with a comprehensive review of existing literature on vibration sensing and railway track monitoring, ensuring the research was built on a solid and up-to-date foundation. Active participation in prototyping and testing various sensor designs helped refine accuracy, reliability, and adaptability.

The role involved designing the vibration sensor casing using Autodesk Inventor and programming the Arduino Uno for sensor data acquisition and analysis. Performance testing on motor bearings was also conducted to validate sensor accuracy under controlled conditions.
